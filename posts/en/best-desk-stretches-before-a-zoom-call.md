Virtual meetings create a peculiar kind of fatigue. You may be sitting still, but your attention is working hard. You are looking at faces, monitoring your own image, processing information, and often moving quickly from one call to the next. A short desk stretch before a Zoom call can change that experience more than people expect. It helps you arrive in the meeting instead of simply falling into it.

Start with a simple seated posture reset. Place both feet on the floor, lengthen through the crown of the head, and let the shoulders drop. Take one full inhale and one long exhale. This creates a physical baseline. Presence often begins with the body feeling slightly less collapsed.

Then try a seated cat-cow for a few rounds. Inhale to broaden the chest and exhale to round gently. This wakes up the spine and refreshes attention. If you have been deep in focused work before the meeting, this kind of movement can help the mind transition more smoothly into conversation.

A shoulder opener is especially effective before calls. Interlace the fingers behind the back or simply draw the shoulders back and widen the chest. This improves the sense of openness in the upper body, which can subtly change how your voice and energy come across. Meetings tend to go better when you do not feel physically compressed.

Finish with two slow breaths, making the exhale slightly longer than the inhale. That small breathing cue helps reduce urgency and can make you feel more grounded before speaking. You do not need a full ritual. Even one minute can help you feel more steady, more present, and less like you are carrying the previous hour into the next conversation.
